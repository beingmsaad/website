Biography

I am a Doctoral Candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Central Florida. At UCF, I am a member of the Security Analytics Research Lab advised by David Mohaisen. I work on the security of distributed systems. More precisely, my work examins the effects of peer-to-peer networking on the security of blockchain systems. Before joining UCF, I did my Undergraduate and Masters in Electrical Engineering from NUST and LUMS in 2014 and 2017, respectively.

My CV can be found here.

Research

Current Research

Currently, I am working on blockchains, covering a range of topics, from primitives and foundations to applications and translations. More precisely, I am perusing three thrusts: 1) foundational and principled research into distributed systems and cryptographic primitives that ensure desirable properties in blockchain systems, such as privacy, security, fairness, high throughput, and concurrency, 2) instrumenting requirements of legacy applications (conventional database solutions, audit logs etc.), and new applications (e.g., IoT, smart systems etc.) for successful translation into a blockchain framework by combining requirements engineering and composable designs, and 3) ensuring sustainability of existing applications in the blockchain ecosystem through active measurements and predictive models. Concurrently, I am also interested in understanding avenues of the abuse of blockchains through systematic attack surface analysis.
